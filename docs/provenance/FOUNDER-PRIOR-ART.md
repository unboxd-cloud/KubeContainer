# Founder Prior Art — Provenance Register

Public, dated statements of the vision predating this repository:
the chain of custody for the ideas themselves. Claim + proof — the
record proves who built it; these entries prove who saw it coming.

The register holds two kinds of provenance, and both count:
prior art (posts stating the vision before it was built — proof the
ideas are the founder's) and prior delivery (posts marking promises
kept for clients and teams over the years — "Promise delivered," dated
each time it was earned). The second kind matters as much as the
first: a reputation is evidence compounded, and a public trail of
delivered promises is exactly the standing this platform was built to
make checkable. LinkedIn supplied the witnesses; this register gives
them custody.

And a third kind, the quietest and most telling: prior consistency —
work from five years back and more, showing the same thought process
held across time. Priority proves who was first; delivery proves who
kept promises; consistency proves it was never a fashion: the same
questions (why is the web disorganized, why is search hard), the same
instincts (file it, prove it, name the payer), the same direction,
years before the era made them obvious. A vision held for five years
is conviction with a timestamp — and trajectory, as the measures
doctrine says, is its own evidence: reputation rewards the curve, not
just the point. Entries of this kind cite the old work, date it, and
name the present doctrine it anticipated — the thought's own
delegation chain, resolving backward to the same mind.

And the final judge is named, because the doctrine demands it: real
world people will evaluate, and they will decide to trust or not.
This register persuades no one; it only makes the evaluation possible.
The platform's deepest rule applies to its founder exactly as to its
boxes: claims are inputs, verdicts are world-owned — and for a person,
the world-test is other people, reading the receipts and rendering
the one verdict no system can automate. Provenance assembled, record
opened, motto on the table: the founder takes the same gate every
artifact here takes — judged by what can be checked, trusted only as
far as the checking holds, one reader at a time. That is not the
system's weakness; it is the entire point.

Discipline (per the NOTICE's host doctrine): a link alone is provenance
held by someone else's platform. Each entry must absorb the substance
into the canon — URL, date, and the claim's text or faithful summary —
so the proof survives the host. Append-only, like every register here.

## Entry format

```
### <date> — <platform> — <one-line claim>
- URL: <link>
- Stated: <the post's relevant text, quoted or faithfully summarized>
- Anticipates: <which principle / doctrine / product it predates>
```
