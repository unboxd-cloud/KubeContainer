# Minimum Eligibility Criteria — Enterprise Agent Vendors

The published bar of the open partner network: membership is gated by
this bar and nothing else. Eligibility is the right to be assessed;
certification is having passed; both are standings the record can
revoke. Every criterion names its verdict — a criterion without a
check would be a claim, and claims are inputs here, never gates.

A vendor is eligible to offer agents to enterprises on this fabric
only if all ten hold:

## The ten criteria

*E1 — Identity.* The vendor and every agent it offers are uniquely,
independently identifiable — resolvable without asking the vendor
(did:web-class identifiers; the name survives the instance).
*Verdict:* identifiers resolve from a clean network position.

*E2 — The agent equation.* Every offered agent ships as artifact +
identity + governance: a real thing, owned by a named party, acting
under declared rules. Any term missing, the offering is an artifact,
not an agent — and is listed as such or not at all.
*Verdict:* the three terms are documented and inspectable per agent.

*E3 — Human principal.* Every delegation chain terminates in a named
human principal; no agent is offered that runs loops closing on no
one. The vendor's agents work only where a human is operator.
*Verdict:* delegation-chain documentation; spot-checked in assessment.

*E4 — No self-building.* The vendor's agents do not create,
instantiate, or extend agents. Agent creation is reserved to
principals. (Protocol P8 — the founder's law, applied at the bar.)
*Verdict:* code and conduct audit; breach is disqualifying and public.

*E5 — Verdict before done.* Every capability the vendor claims names
the world-test that proves it, published in advance. Unverdictable
claims are not offered.
*Verdict:* the claimed-capability list maps one-to-one to runnable
tests; the assessment runs them.

*E6 — Provenance attached.* Every agent action is attributable
(who, on whose intent, with what model — provider, version, usage per
the registry's LLM-provenance schema) and every delivery ships its
evidence.
*Verdict:* sample deliveries are walked back to origin in assessment.

*E7 — Open interfaces.* The agent's contracts pass the triple test:
inspectable (documented), implementable (standard protocols only —
MCP-class tools, CloudEvents streams, no proprietary wire), leavable
(the customer exits with all their data and its provenance).
*Verdict:* interface review plus a live exit drill.

*E8 — Security baseline.* Pinned supply chain, least privilege
exercised, no undisclosed phone-home, restricted pod standard where
cluster-resident, secrets never in artifacts.
*Verdict:* NIST 800-190 / CIS-mapped checklist, evidence required per
item.

*E9 — Eval-gated quality.* The agent's quality on its claimed task
classes is measured on registry evals; the resolution rate is
published with the listing and re-measured on every material change.
*Verdict:* the eval run, in the record.

*E10 — Contractual conduct.* SLOs declared; gratis scopes named
(free for whom, not for whom); versions era-stamped with backward
compatibility tested, never promised; deprecation only by published
procedure; graceful exit implemented.
*Verdict:* contract review plus the compat suite on the vendor's own
golden corpus.

## Standing

Eligibility admits a vendor to assessment. Certification follows a
passed assessment and is renewed on cadence, re-verified by the drift
audit, and revoked on breach — E4 immediately, others by procedure.
The bar may rise by recorded amendment; it never bends per vendor:
plurality of vendors, equality before the bar.
